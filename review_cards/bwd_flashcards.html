Basic Web Design
Flashcards

text-align
A style that sets the horizontal alignment of text. Can be left, center, right, or justify
font-weight
A style that sets the boldeness of text. Can be words or number: Lighter (100, 200, 300), normal (400, 500), bold (600, 700) or bolder (800, 900)
font-style
A style that sets the style of text. Can be normal, italic, or oblique
padding
A style that adds space within an element with px or %. Can also be padding-top, padding-bottom, padding-left, or padding-right to be more specific. Can also be shorthanded in the format top, right, bottom, left, in the format top/bottom, left/right
margin
A style that adds space within an element with px or %. Can also be auto, which horizontally center items automatically. Can also be margin-top, margin-bottom, margin-left, or margin-right to be more specific. Can also be shorthanded in the format top, right, bottom, left, or in the format top/bottom, left/right
border
A style that is shorthand for the 3 border styles. Written in the format border-width, border-style, border-color. Can also be border-top, border-bottom, border-left, or border-right to be more specific
<transition>
Makes a transition for an element by combining the 2 transition codes. Goes the format transition-property, transition-duration
<animation-name>
The name of an animation to be played for an element. Name and effect given by keyframes
